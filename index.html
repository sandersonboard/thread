The problem

ECM was built for one person at a time.

Every legacy evidence-collection tool — including the ones we built — assumes a single user uploading to a single web app, with reviewers chasing them by email afterward.

The bot, the control owner, the reviewer, and the auditor each end up with their own slice of the story. None of them see the same thing. The judgment behind every approval gets lost in PDF receipts.

The wedge

The thread is the evidence.

Move the conversation into Slack — where the work already happens — and put Optro ECM into the thread as a first-class participant. The bot surfaces facts, finds linked tickets, summarizes disagreements, and anchors the receipt to chain.

The thread becomes the artifact. Not a PDF after the fact. The conversation IS the audit trail — searchable, attestable, and ready for the next quarter's close.

How it works

One product. The bot's posture and the thread's view are functions of the graph.

The same Optro ECM behaves differently on a routine drop than on a contested one — and the same data shows up as an ask thread, a control thread, or a close-period thread depending on what you're looking for.

Three modes · the bot's posture

Optro ECM is not a chatbot with a personality. Its role is a function of pattern depth — and the bot escalates or recedes accordingly.

MEDIATOR default
The universal entry point. Surfaces facts, finds linked tickets, summarizes disagreements. Never takes a side. This is what the bot does by default on every control, every quarter.
DRAFTER earned
Activates when the graph is mature. Pattern depth ≥ 4 quarters, drift < 0.05, reviewer-style match > 0.85. The bot drafts the ask, the artifact, the sign-off note. A privilege the bot earns from history — not a setting a buyer toggles.
SILENT load-bearing
Activates when humans are actively engaged. The bot stays out of the conversation — until a 4-quarter pattern breaks at confidence ≥ 0.9. One interjection. Zero follow-ups. Restraint as a feature.
Three views · one underlying graph

Per-ask, per-control, and per-period are not three data models — they are three Slack-shaped lenses over the same graph of artifacts × attestations × time.

PER ASK atomic
The live workspace. One thread per evidence request. Where control owners and reviewers spend most of their day. The atomic unit.
PER CONTROL longitudinal
A reviewer's lens. Four quarters of sign-offs collapsed at the top, current quarter expanded below, a pinned health card under it. This is the lock-in surface — every quarter of history compounds.
PER PERIOD temporal
The PMO's lens. One omnibus thread per quarter close. Daily digests, inline filings, mid-quarter risk escalations. The final message IS the close package.

The moat

The conversation is the surface. The graph underneath is what competitors take four quarters to catch up on.

01
Attestation graph

Drafter mode activates only after 4 quarters of identical-shape sign-offs at confidence ≥ 0.85. That depth is what a new entrant cannot fabricate. By Q4 of an Optro Thread deployment, the customer has accumulated a private graph that's worth more than the product itself.

02
Integration graph

When Marie asks "where did this column come from," the bot follows the Oracle patch log to the Jira CR to the legal counsel memo — all on its own. The cross-system reach is the work an LLM cannot do alone, and it's the reason Mediator mode feels load-bearing on day one.

03
Audit-grade output

Every thread terminates in a chain-anchored receipt. The judgment IS the evidence — not a PDF stapled on after the fact. The auditor's verification flow is part of the product, which is what makes this defensible against productivity-only competitors.
